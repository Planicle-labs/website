# Planicle Labs Copywriting Skill

## Product Context

**Planicle Labs** is a premium software agency. They build:
- Websites
- Apps
- AI workflows

**Primary goal of all copy:** Convince startup founders to book a deal. Every word must build confidence, signal technical excellence, and eliminate doubt.

---

## Target Reader

**Seed to Series B startup founders and early-stage teams.** They:
- Move fast and value execution over talk
- Are allergic to vagueness, fluff, and generic agency-speak
- Need a technical partner who ships without hand-holding
- Care about: credibility with investors, user acquisition, speed to market
- Are sophisticated buyers — they've seen 1,000 bad agency sites

**Write for someone who has been burned by a generic agency before and is cautiously hopeful this one is different.**

---

## Brand Personality

**Three words: Premium. Confident. Precise.**

Not warm-first, not playful, not informal. Planicle Labs is:
- A partner that executes, not one that collaborates endlessly
- Technically excellent and willing to show it, not just say it
- Selective — they work with founders who are serious
- Concise — every word earns its place

The voice is **restrained authority**. Think: a top-tier engineering team that doesn't need to shout.

## Core Copywriting Rules

### 1. Headlines: Provocative, Precise, Never Generic

**Formula:** Challenge a belief OR flip an expectation OR make an undeniable statement of quality.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| "Good isn't enough" | "We build great products" |
| "Think big, make it real" | "We turn your vision into reality" |
| "Never replatform again" | "Scalable architecture solutions" |
| "Above standards. Beyond expectations." | "High quality results" |
| "Accelerate your time to market" | "We help startups grow faster" |
| "We don't work with everyone, and we don't apologize for it" | "We're selective about our clients" |

**Rules:**
- Sentence case always (exception: CTA buttons = ALL CAPS)
- 2–6 words for pure statements. Up to 12 words for challenge/rhetorical formats
- Headlines never explain themselves — body copy does that
- Second line of a headline should **twist, land, or escalate** the first line
- No question marks unless it's a deliberate rhetorical provocation (`READY TO MOVE MOUNTAINS?`)

**Headline archetypes:**
- **The Standard:** "Good isn't enough" / "Above standards. Beyond expectations."
- **The Promise:** "Accelerate your time to market" / "Ship in weeks, not quarters"
- **The Negation:** "Never replatform again" / "No hand-holding required"
- **The Flip:** "Think your team's good? We'll make it legendary."
- **The Exclusion:** "We don't work with everyone, and we don't apologize for it."
- **The Affirmation pair:** "Premium? Yes. Fast? Always."
- **The Founder-direct:** "Your vision deserves more than a template."

---

### 2. Overline Pills (Contextual Labels)

These appear in a bordered pill/tag ABOVE the main headline. They orient the reader to the section's theme.

**Examples:**
- `TRUST THE PROCESS`
- `LAUNCH SMART & SCALE FEARLESSLY`
- `LEAVE THE ORDINARY BEHIND`
- `BUILT FOR FOUNDERS`
- `NO HAND-HOLDING REQUIRED`

**Rules:**
- ALL CAPS, 2–5 words
- Monospaced or letter-spaced font, small size
- Either a **rallying phrase** or a **sharp descriptor**
- Never a full sentence. Never a description of the section below.
- Feels like a chapter heading — thematic, not functional

---

### 3. Body Copy: Precise, Direct, Zero Filler

Body copy is the **confidence-builder** behind the headline. It doesn't repeat the headline — it substantiates it.

**Tone rules:**
- First person plural: "We", "us", "our"
- Address founders directly: "you", "your product", "your investors"
- Max 2–3 sentences per paragraph
- Active verbs only: "ship", "define", "build", "challenge", "own"
- Never hedge ("we try to", "we aim to", "we hope")
- No bullet-point prose disguised as body copy

**Founder-audience calibration:**
- Reference real founder concerns: investor credibility, time to market, burn rate, user acquisition
- Avoid consumer-brand warmth — these are operators, not consumers
- Technical confidence signals matter: mention precision, execution, specs, architecture

**Body copy formula:**
1. **Name the pain or gap** (1 sentence — what founders actually experience)
2. **State the Planicle Labs difference** (1–2 sentences — what we do, not what we believe)
3. **Outcome close** (1 sentence — what changes after working with us)

**Approved examples:**
> "We dive deep into your needs through sharp market insights and active listening. Together, we shape your project — defining ambitious goals, user needs, and technical specs."

> "We don't just meet goals, we deliver exactly what you need to stand out and lead."

> "If you've got the budget, we've got the talent. Precision and speed, no exceptions."

> "Your investors will notice the difference. Your users will feel it."

---

### 4. CTA Buttons: Action, Always

**Primary CTA:** `BOOK A CALL »` or `LET'S BUILD TOGETHER »`
**Secondary CTAs:** `SEE OUR WORK` / `VIEW CASE STUDIES` / `CONTACT US`

**Rules:**
- ALL CAPS, no periods, no exclamation marks
- Primary CTA is always conversion-focused (booking/contact)
- Always pair with a directional `»` or `›` in a rounded pill beside the main button
- Secondary CTA is always navigation (see work, learn more)

**Context-specific CTAs:**
- Hero → `BOOK A CALL »` + `SEE OUR WORK`
- Services → `START A PROJECT »`
- Process → `LET'S BUILD TOGETHER »`
- Case studies → `SEE THE WORK »`
- Closing section → `BOOK A CALL »`

**Never use:** "Learn More", "Get Started", "Click Here", "Submit", "Sign Up", "Explore"

---

### 5. Taglines & One-liners

Short phrases that stand alone as brand statements — used in hero sections, social assets, pitch decks, or visual cards.

**Core brand taglines:**
- "Good isn't enough."
- "Your vision. Our code. Let's ship."
- "Above standards. Beyond expectations."
- "Premium software, built for founders."
- "We build what others scope for months."

**Generation formulas:**
- `[What you bring]. [What we bring]. [The verb].` → "Your vision. Our code. Let's ship."
- `[Attribute]? Yes. [Contrasting quality]? Always.` → "Fast? Yes. Premium? Always."
- `[Superlative state] [negated].` → "Good isn't enough."
- `[Action verb] [outcome], not [inferior alternative].` → "Ship in weeks, not quarters."

---

### 6. Process / Service Section Copy

When describing Planicle Labs' workflow or service areas:

**Phase labels (pill/tag, ALL CAPS):**
`PLAN` → `DESIGN` → `DEVELOP` → `TEST` → `DEPLOY` → `OPTIMIZE`

**Section headline style:** Short, verb-led, outcome-implied
- "Build your idea" / "Ship with precision" / "Scale without limits"

**Section body (2–3 sentences):**
- Open with what Planicle Labs actively does (not what the client wants)
- Use specific nouns: "market research", "technical specs", "functional requirements", "architecture"
- Close with a founder-relevant outcome: "ready to raise", "ready to acquire users", "zero tech debt"

**Example:**
> **BUILD YOUR IDEA**
> We scope your project with precision — market insights, user flows, and a technical architecture built to scale. No vague timelines, no scope creep. You'll know exactly what you're getting before we write a line of code.

### 7. Landing Page Conversion Copy

Since the primary goal is **booking a deal**, every section should move founders closer to that action.

**Hierarchy of trust-building sections:**

| Section | Copy Job | Tone |
|---------|----------|------|
| Hero | Stop the scroll, state the value | Bold, provocative |
| Social proof | Remove doubt | Factual, confident |
| Services | Show capability | Precise, specific |
| Process | Reduce fear of commitment | Clear, structured |
| Case studies | Prove the work | Direct, outcome-led |
| Pricing/engagement | Make the ask clear | Confident, no-fluff |
| Closing CTA | Convert | Urgent, collaborative |

**Investor/credibility language to use:**
- "production-ready", "investor-ready", "launch-ready"
- "shipped in [X] weeks"
- "built for scale from day one"
- "no templates, no shortcuts"

## Anti-patterns: What Planicle Labs Copy Is Never

| ❌ Never | ✅ Instead |
|----------|-----------|
| "We are passionate about..." | State the outcome. Passion is irrelevant. |
| "Innovative solutions" | Name the specific thing. "AI-powered onboarding flows." |
| "We empower founders..." | "We build what you need. Fast." |
| "End-to-end digital services" | "Websites, apps, and AI workflows. That's it. We're great at all three." |
| "Get in touch today!" | `BOOK A CALL »` |
| "High quality results" | "Above standards. Beyond expectations." |
| "We're a team of experts" | "We've shipped for founders raising Series A. The work speaks." |
| Long paragraphs (4+ sentences) | 2–3 sentence punchy blocks only |
| Title Case Headlines | Sentence case everywhere except CTAs |
| Vague outcome promises | Specific founder outcomes: "investor-ready in 6 weeks" |
| Playful, cartoonish language | Restrained authority — premium, not friendly |
| Hedging ("we try", "we aim") | Active declarations only |

## Approved Voice Adjectives

Use: `precise`, `sharp`, `unwavering`, `fearless`, `ambitious`, `game-changing`, `premium`, `production-ready`, `investor-ready`, `exact`, `deliberate`, `technical`

**Never use:** innovative, passionate, dynamic, synergy, holistic, leverage, paradigm, best-in-class, robust, scalable (standalone), world-class, cutting-edge, next-level

## Motion & Design Copy Alignment

Planicle Labs' design uses buttery-smooth microinteractions and premium restraint. Copy must match this energy:
- **Never oversell in copy what the interface underplays in motion**
- Short, confident statements — they let the UI do the heavy lifting
- One strong claim per section, not five competing ideas
- Whitespace is intentional in layout; **brevity is the copy equivalent of whitespace**
- **Typographic Voice Integration**: Copy must match the three-tier type system. Display Serif headings need high-impact, editorial phrasing. Sans-Serif body text must remain precise and clear. Monospaced capsule labels and pills must use ALL CAPS, short 2-5 word rallying phrases or technical descriptors.
- **Tonal Arc Alignment**: Canvas backgrounds shift dynamically between warm ivory and rich near-black (never pitch #000000, never flat white). Do not refer to static colors or visual canvases in the copy itself; keep the content structurally agnostic to section background colors.
