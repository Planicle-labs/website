# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Seed to Series B startup founders and early-stage teams. They move fast, value execution over talk, and are allergic to vagueness. They've seen hundreds of agency sites and been burned by at least one generic shop. They arrive cautiously hopeful, scanning for signals of real competence. Their context: evaluating whether Planicle Labs is the technical partner who can ship their product without hand-holding, on a timeline that respects their burn rate.

## Product Purpose

Marketing landing page for Planicle Labs, a premium software agency that builds websites, apps, and AI workflows for startups. The site is the primary proof of competency: if the interface itself doesn't feel exceptional, the pitch fails before the copy lands. Primary conversion: booked call. Secondary goal: brand awareness and credibility that founders reference when talking to co-founders and investors.

## Brand Personality

**Premium. Confident. Precise.**

The voice is restrained authority. Not warm-first, not playful, not informal. Planicle Labs is a partner that executes, technically excellent and willing to show it. Every word earns its place. The emotional target is calm confidence: "they clearly know what they're doing, I trust them."

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with gradient blobs, floating UI mockups, and "trusted by 10,000+ companies" social proof bars
- Overly playful agency sites with cartoon vector illustrations and bouncy animations
- Cluttered interfaces that try to say everything at once
- Cookie-cutter dark-mode dashboards passed off as agency portfolios
- Any layout that reads as a template rather than a deliberate design choice

## Design Principles

1. **The interface is the pitch.** The site itself must be the strongest proof of Planicle Labs' craft. If a founder can't feel the quality before reading a word of copy, the design has failed.

2. **Credibility through restraint.** Confidence doesn't shout. Earn trust through precision, intentional whitespace, and considered typography rather than spectacle or visual noise.

3. **Tonal arc, not tonal monotony.** The page transitions between light and dark registers as the founder scrolls, creating an emotional rhythm. The hero breathes in light; deeper sections shift to softer darks. Never pitch black, never flat white throughout.

4. **Typography carries the voice.** A three-tier type system — high-contrast editorial serif for display, clean sans-serif for body, technical monospace for labels and pills — is the primary expressive tool. Type does the heavy lifting; illustrations are minimal and subordinate to the editorial layout.

5. **Earn the scroll.** Each section must give the founder a reason to keep going. The page builds a case, section by section, that culminates in the only reasonable action: booking a call.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline. All typography must meet 4.5:1 contrast ratio against backgrounds. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by disabling animations. Semantic HTML with proper ARIA attributes on interactive elements.
